Our Story

David Augustine Kisukwa leads the Mfangano Youth Mindset Innovation on a remote island in Kenya's Lake Victoria, where he has been supporting 50 orphaned and vulnerable children with basic needs like food, education, and healthcare.

Working within the Suba and Luo speaking communities, Kisukwa has developed a comprehensive understanding of what these children need - not just shelter, but trauma-informed care, vocational training, and community integration.

Now he seeks to formalize and expand this grassroots effort into a permanent orphanage facility with proper infrastructure and sustainable programming. His detailed budget and holistic approach suggest someone who has moved beyond good intentions to practical experience, ready to scale community-driven solutions into lasting institutional change.

Core Values & Beliefs

Holistic Development

MYMI believes that providing basic shelter alone is insufficient for vulnerable children. True transformation requires simultaneous attention to education, healthcare, psychological healing, and social integration.

Trauma-Informed Care

We recognize that orphaned and vulnerable children carry emotional wounds that must be addressed through professional counseling and therapeutic approaches, not simply overlooked or expected to heal naturally.

Community Ownership

We believe sustainable change requires deep engagement with local parents, guardians, and community leaders. The community must be empowered to take increasing responsibility for the project's long-term success.

Dignity Through Self-Reliance

Rather than creating dependency through charity alone, MYMI focuses on vocational training and life skills to produce self-reliant, responsible, and contributing members of society.

Cultural Respect

We intentionally honor the unique cultural heritage of the Suba and Luo communities, ensuring that care respects local traditions and values rather than imposing outside cultural norms.

Transparency and Accountability

We maintain rigorous monitoring, evaluation, and documentation standards, believing in measurable outcomes and responsible stewardship of all resources.

Long-Term Sustainability

We prioritize capacity-building and sustainable funding models, recognizing that short-term interventions without lasting support systems ultimately fail the children they are meant to serve.

Current Goals and Needs

Land Acquisition

MYMI requires vast land to purchase for establishing a permanent orphanage facility to replace current temporary arrangements.

Facility Construction

The organization needs to build or renovate safe, child-friendly buildings that meet regulatory standards for housing 50+ children.

Annual Operating Budget

MYMI requires approximately $10,000 USD yearly to cover food, school fees, healthcare, and staff salaries for current operations.

Professional Staff:

The organization needs qualified caregivers, teachers, counselors, and social workers trained in trauma-informed care and child protection.

Educational Resources

School notebooks, recreational programs, and transportation for educational activities remain unfunded and need immediate support.

Medical Care Access

Regular healthcare services, vaccinations, and emergency medical support require ongoing funding and local healthcare partnerships.

Sustainable Funding Model

MYMI needs diverse revenue streams including grants, donations, and income-generating activities to ensure long-term viability.

Community Partnerships

The organization requires stronger relationships with local government, NGOs, and community leaders to leverage additional resources and support.

Monitoring Systems

MYMI needs robust tracking and evaluation tools to measure child development outcomes and program effectiveness.

Capacity Building

Local staff and community members require training to gradually assume greater responsibility for project management and operations.
